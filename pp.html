1 Conversion

1.1 Momentum

Relevant formula: \[ E = pc \] for massless particle, so reinsert \(c\) by multiplication.

1.2 Mass

\[ E = mc^2\] Multiply by \(c^2\)

1.3 Length

Relevant formula is the reduced Compton Wavelength (I think): \[ \lambda = \frac{\hbar}{mc}\]

But basically length is usually given in \( [GeV]^{-1}\), so multiply with \(\hbar c\). A useful conversion factor is \(\hbar c = 0.197 GeV fm\)

1.4 Time

By dimensional analysis: \(\hbar [Js], c [ms^{-1}], GeV [J]\), so: \[ \hbar GeV^{-1} = [Js \cdot J^{-1}] = s\]

2 Four Vectors and Invariants

2.1 Invariant Formula

Invariant formula which equates energy, momentum and mass gives: \[ E^2 - p^2c^2 = m^2 c^4\]

2.2 Momentum Four-Vector

For some bizarre reason the four-vector is defined to be: \[ P = [E, cp_x, cp_y, cp_z]\] The inner product: \[ P^2 = E^2 - p^2c^2\]

2.3 Invariant Mass

Defined as \(W = P/c^2\) so: \[ W^2 c^4 = \left(\sum^N_i E_i\right)^2 - \left(\sum^N_i \hat{p}_i\right)^2\]

2.4 Pair of Particles Invariant Mass

\[ (P_1 + P_2)\cdot (P_1 + P_2)\] Expand accordingly and note: \[ P_1^2 = E_1^2 -c^2|\hat{p}|^2\] If mass is negligible, by invariant formula: \[ E2 - p2c2 = 02c2 ∴ E2 = p2c2]

2.5 Collisions

2.5.1 Colliding Beam

\[ E_{CM} = 2E_{\text{beam}}\]

2.5.2 Fixed Target

\[ E_{CM} = \sqrt{2E_\text{beam}m_\text{target}}\]

2.5.3 Angle

The COM energy is: \[ W = \sqrt{2E_1E_2(1-\cos\theta)}\]

2.6 Assumptions

  • The beam energy is usually much larger than the mass

4 Potentials and Amplitudes

4.1 Relativistic Scattering Amplitudes

\[ \cal{M} = \frac{g^2\hbar^2c^2}{p^2-M^2_xc^4} \] where \(p) is the 4-momentum exchanged and \(M_X\) is the mass of the exchange particle. You can be told that \( g_{EM} = g_\text{WEAK}\). Take the square for the probability (as with QM).

7 Detectors and Radiation

7.1 Detector Properties and Construction

  • Position trackers are placed close to interaction to measure decay length (B-hadrons have only a few mm)
  • High granularity gives ability to reconstruct crowded events
  • Magnetic field causes particles to follow helical trajectories, so the momentum can be measured based on the radius of curvature
  • Hadronic interaction length is 10+cm so placed deeper than EM calorimeterd
  • Muons travel the farthest and can be detected by scintillator on the outer layer

7.2 Bethe-Bloch

Sketch \(\frac{dE}{dx}\) over \(\beta\gamma\) (which are the relativistic constants. Properties of the graph:

  • Steep fall as \(\beta\gamma\) increases at low speeds
  • Broad minimum - "minimum ionising" region
  • Slow "logarithmic" rise at large \(\beta\gamma\)

7.3 Line Widths and Lifetimes

The line width is one over lifetime, and lifetime is proportional to one over mass to the power of 5. \[ \tau = \left(\frac{1}{m}\right)^5\]

9 Symmetries

9.1 Helicity

Helicity is the projection of spin on its momentum.

It is positive and right-handed if the spin is aligned with its momentum. It is negative and left-handed if the spin is in the other direction.

  • Neutrinos are left-handed
  • Anti-neutrinos are right-handed

10 Nuclear Physics

10.1 Half-Life

Nuclear activity is defined as: \[ A = \frac{dN}{dt} = \frac{N}{\tau}\] Integrating (I think) gives: \[ N(t) = N_0 e^{-\frac{t}{\tau}}\] The half life is given by: \[ N(T_\frac12) = 0.5N_0 \] Some algebra gives: \[ T_\frac12 = \tau ln(2) \]

10.2 SEMF and Decays

10.2.1 The \(\beta^-\) decay

This is: \[ n \rightarrow p + e^- \bar{\nu}_e\] This decay is allowed if: \[M(Z,A) > M(Z+1,A)\]

10.2.2 The \(\beta^+\) decay

This is: \[ p \rightarrow n + e^+ + \nu_e\] This is allowed if: \[ M(Z,A) > M(Z-1,A)+2m_e\]

10.2.3 Electron Capture

This is: \[ p + e^- \rightarrow n + \nu_e\] This is allowed if: \[ M(Z,A) > M(Z-1,A)+\epsilon\] Where \(\epsilon\) is the excitation energy of the atomic shell of the captured electron.

10.3 Shell Model

10.3.1 Nomenclature

The label is: \[(nl_J)^N\] Where \( J = L+S\) Each nucleon can occupy:

  • \( n = 1,2,3,...\)
  • \(l = 0,1,2,3,...n-1\)
  • There are \(2l+1\) substates
  • Each \(l\) level can hold \(2J+1\) nucleons

10.3.2 Predictions

  1. Spins

    The spin is determined by the unpaired nucleon, otherwise 0 if all nucleons are paired. If it is an odd-odd nuclei, the spin lies on: \[ |J_P-J_N| \leq s_N \leq J_P+J_N\]

  2. Parity

    Intrinsic parities of proton and neutron are \(+1\), and the parity due to angular momentum is \(P = (-1)^l\), and as it is a multiplicative quantum number: \[ P_\text{nucleus} = P_\text{last-proton} \times P_\text{last-neutron}\]
