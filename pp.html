1 Conversion

1.1 Momentum

Relevant formula: \[ E = pc \] for massless particle, so reinsert \(c\) by multiplication.

1.2 Mass

\[ E = mc^2\] Multiply by \(c^2\)

1.3 Length

Relevant formula is the reduced Compton Wavelength (I think): \[ \lambda = \frac{\hbar}{mc}\]

But basically length is usually given in \( [GeV]^{-1}\), so multiply with \(\hbar c\). A useful conversion factor is \(\hbar c = 0.197 GeV fm\)

1.4 Time

By dimensional analysis: \(\hbar [Js], c [ms^{-1}], GeV [J]\), so: \[ \hbar GeV^{-1} = [Js \cdot J^{-1}] = s\]

2 Four Vectors and Invariants

2.1 Invariant Formula

Invariant formula which equates energy, momentum and mass gives: \[ E^2 - p^2c^2 = m^2 c^4\]

2.2 Momentum Four-Vector

For some bizarre reason the four-vector is defined to be: \[ P = [E, cp_x, cp_y, cp_z]\] The inner product: \[ P^2 = E^2 - p^2c^2\]

2.3 Invariant Mass

Defined as \(W = P/c^2\) so: \[ W^2 c^4 = \left(\sum^N_i E_i\right)^2 - \left(\sum^N_i \hat{p}_i\right)^2\]

2.4 Collisions

2.4.1 Colliding Beam

\[ E_{CM} = 2E_{\text{beam}}\]

2.4.2 Fixed Target

\[ E_{CM} = \sqrt{2E_\text{beam}m_\text{target}}\]

5 Properties

5.1 Line Widths and Lifetimes

The line width is one over lifetime, and lifetime is proportional to one over mass to the power of 5. \[ \tau = \left(\frac{1}{m}\right)^5\]

6 Interaction

6.1 Range of forces

  • Photon: infinite

  • Z-boson: 0.002 fm

7 Symmetries

7.1 Helicity

Helicity is the projection of spin on its momentum.

It is positive and right-handed if the spin is aligned with its momentum. It is negative and left-handed if the spin is in the other direction.

  • Neutrinos are left-handed
  • Anti-neutrinos are right-handed

8 Nuclear Physics

8.1 Half-Life

Nuclear activity is defined as: \[ A = \frac{dN}{dt} = \frac{N}{\tau}\] Integrating (I think) gives: \[ N(t) = N_0 e^{-\frac{t}{\tau}}\] The half life is given by: \[ N(T_\frac12) = 0.5N_0 \] Some algebra gives: \[ T_\frac12 = \tau ln(2) \]
